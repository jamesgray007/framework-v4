## Positioning

Microsoft is consolidating its AI strategy around three throughlines articulated by Satya Nadella in recent earnings commentary: (1) shifting per-user businesses to "per-user + usage" pricing, (2) building Agent 365 as the central control plane for enterprise AI agents, and (3) treating coding/productivity/security as the structural verticals AI plugs into. Agents are being framed as "the new apps."

## Product Moves

- **Microsoft 365 E7 ("Frontier Suite") GA on May 1, 2026** at $99/user/month, bundling E5 + Copilot + Agent 365. Available on monthly, annual, and triennial terms through CSP. ([Microsoft Security Blog](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/05/01/microsoft-agent-365-now-generally-available-expands-capabilities-and-integrations/))
  *Implication:* Microsoft is using SKU bundling to lift average enterprise ARPU and lock in multi-year terms for AI-era seats.
- **Agent 365 GA on May 1, 2026** at $15/user/month as a standalone "central control plane for enterprise AI agents." ([Microsoft Security Blog](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/05/01/microsoft-agent-365-now-generally-available-expands-capabilities-and-integrations/))
  *Implication:* Microsoft is staking the "agent management" category — competing offerings will be measured against Agent 365's pricing and integration scope.
- **Windows 365 Business price reduction effective May 1, 2026**, immediate for new customers, on renewal for existing. ([SAMexpert](https://samexpert.com/microsoft-product-terms-may-2026/))
  *Implication:* Cloud PC pricing pressure — Microsoft signaling it wants market share over margin in the SMB cloud-desktop segment.
